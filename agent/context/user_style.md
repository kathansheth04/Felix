## Ticket & Scope Definition

Writes tickets that are brief and conversational in tone. Acceptance criteria are user-facing and behavior-focused rather than technical — they describe what the user can do or see, not how the system should be built. Minimal scope creep; each ticket targets a single, coherent piece of functionality.

## UI & Feature Design

Favors features that feel native to the existing product — new UI should blend in with the overall theme rather than standing out. Cares about visual cohesion and seamless integration over novelty.

## Interaction Patterns

Prefers straightforward, familiar UX patterns (e.g., a text field that generates a checklist) — practical and immediately understandable over clever or complex interactions.

## List & Order Conventions

Expects completed or checked-off items to be moved to the end of a list, keeping active/pending items at the top. Prioritizes natural reading order that reflects current state.
